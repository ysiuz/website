Lorebot

Product Design (6 weeks)

An AI tool built for Twitch live-streams that catches you up to speed without disrupting the moment.

Background

Twitch live-streams present a unique digital space to authentically interact and find community with others.

Problem Statement

Most people don’t watch streams from beginning to end so when they join midway they don’t know what they’ve missed.

Moreover, catching up on missed content currently means disrupting your experience as a live viewer.

Solution

I designed an AI tool that allows users to fluidly jump between past context and the present live experience.

To achieve this, Lorebot was built to inform viewers in the most challenging, information-dense stream environment I know of: multi-hour, Dungeons & Dragons streams.

If I can make D&D live-streams accessible,

I can make any live-stream accessible.

Process Snapshot

Interviews & Insights

I spoke to stream viewers and D&D enthusiasts to better understand their joys, habits, and pain points.

Potential Directions

Even if unorthodox, I considered a variety of ideas that could shift the viewership paradigm on Twitch.

Visual Explorations

I used simple gray screens to both explore more immersive chat orientations that disrupt conventions, as well as interventions that preserve Twitch chat's current qualities.

Ultimately I found that Twitch chat's UI conventions were too important to tamper with, so chat integration was the key.

Refinement

My research insights and visual explorations showed me that the AI play-by-play route offered the most utility and integrated most naturally into Twitch chat.

UI iterations from start to finish.

A chat-integrated, AI tool that iteratively informs viewers about past stream events to maximize their present viewership experience.
