Stone Does
Not Speak


April - September 2024


InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop, Figma

Advised by Kelsey Elder, Kelsey Dusenka & Brett Yasko

Featuring the work of Dr. Vishvesh Kandolkar & Paulo Verala Gomes

For 419 years the Basilica of Bom Jesus has stood as an iconic cultural heritage site within the distinct Goan region of India.
My task was to map the church's lesser known stories, and highlight the communities that continue to fight for its survival.
To accomplish this I wrote and designed a 55 page, saddle stitch booklet that documents the basilica’s core stories, and developed a brief web component that serves as a “FAQ” for the heritage site.

Two scripts, one voice.

Across the booklet English and Hindi are given equal page space so that local Goans and Indian tourists alike can understand the Basilica’s story.

*It should be noted much of the Hindi translation is currently incomplete and subject to change.

Evocative materials
& easy assembly.

Velum is utlized on the outer cover to highlight the drastic deplastering of the church and its structural changes over time.

The booklet itself is otherwise entirely composed of folded 8.5" x 11" newsprint, stapled together. This both leans into's the religious and historical roots and conventions of the story, and makes the production of the booklets a breeze :)

Creating space
for the people.
