Stone Does Not Speak

Publication Design

Mapping the essential stories of a centuries-old Goan church, with community at the heart of it all.

Print & web.

For 419 years the Basilica of Bom Jesus has endured as an iconic cultural heritage site within the distinct Goan region of India.
To tell it's story I wrote and designed a 55 page, saddle-stitch booklet that documents the basilica’s core ideas, and developed a brief web component that serves as a “FAQ” for the heritage site.

Two scripts, one voice.

Across the booklet English and Hindi are given equal page space so that local Goans and Indian tourists alike can understand the Basilica’s story.
*Much of the Hindi translation is currently incomplete and subject to change.

Evocative materials
& efficient assembly.

A translucent vellum cover highlights the church's dramatic deplastering, while the interior of the book uses grainy, cream newsprint for its warmth and simplicity. Designed for easy production, the booklet invites all to hold and hear its message.

Community at the core.

The basilica is ultimately a hollow structure. It remains, and steeped in history and significance, only because people chose to pour their time and energy into it. They are the heart of the basilica.
