StuCo ONGOING*

Brand System

Tea culture, flags, personal finance, Taylor Swift, Genshin Impact, — you name it, and CMU’s Student College has a student-run class teaching it.
So... how can a single visual identity reflect the colorful, haphazard, but earnest nature of the student body and our interests?

Time:

July 2023 - Present

Role:

Branding

Tools:

Ai, Ae, Figma

Team:

Isis Berymon (StuCo Design Chair)

A Fresh, Sticky Visual Direction

The previous StuCo logo, though fun, didn’t do a great job at indicating that StuCo actually is a short-form for two different words. Our early work then focused on highlighting both the “Student” and the “College”, as well as better matching the lively, scrappy nature of the organization. Through iteration guided and assisted by Isis Berymon, the sticky-note motif emerged as a way to bring color, funk, and modularity to the brand.

All Caps At All Times

Inspired by the previous logo's tall typography, as well as the proud and funky nature of CMU's students, we wanted to choose sans-serif typefaces that express themselves with strength. Akshar struck the right tone and looks great in all caps, however on it's own it can be monotonous. Unbounded brings width and edge that compliments Akshar, hopefully creating intrigue.

CMU Colors...
With A Twist

Carnegie Mellon has a well established palette of colors, predominantly consisting of reds, golds, greens, blues, and teals. We took these classic CMU colors, and injected a bit of StuCo funk into them, even giving them fun campus-related names. Pink was added because it just feels right*
