Problem

Social media algorithms are opaque

It’s hard to grasp just how narrow our digital view can be-or how far it may diverge from the experiences of others.

2. Openings news is losing traction at current reporting speed

Restaurant openings were often bundled into monthly lists, making coverage too slow. We need to report with greater urgency to keep stories timely and engaging.

3. Newsroom tipping is underutilized

Local coverage relies on reader tips, but our current system is fragmented and underused. We need a more intuitive, accessible way for readers to contribute news.

Current tip submission page

Pertinence, immediacy, transparency

Solution

A reader-driven feed for local openings and closings

Shaped by local tips, and content filters, the feed delivers quick, relevant news.

Information Architecture

I identified the need for 4 core flows for a functional product, which include a scrolling feed, onboarding, tip submission, and feed filtering.

Core Experiences

Scrolling Feed

Post Types - News at multiple scales

Snippets, expandable stories, tip responses, sponsored posts, and ads coexist, balancing immediacy with depth and monetization.

Expandable post

Feed Layout - Familiar news but faster

A scrolling feed inspired by social apps, emphasizing writers as trusted voices rather than relying on polished visuals and lengthy stories.

Map Modal: Adding spatial context

Beyond just listing an address, a visual map intuitively lets readers know where a business is, and if they'd be willing to travel to it.

Onboarding

Introducing a news feed, tipping, and content filtering

Intro modals and a dedicated filters page explain the concept of a fast, reader-informed news feed.

Introducing filters through a dedicated page

Rather than relying on temporary modals, the onboarding points users to the filters page, creating a consistent, reusable place to manage preferences.

Before

Onboarding directly prompts initial filtering in one-off modals.

After

Onboarding modal prompts user to visit filters page to filter feed.

Tip Submission

Incentivising productive reader contributions

Readers can quickly draft, submit, and choose visibility for their tips, creating transparency in how the newsroom may respond to their information.

Prioritizing clarity over brevity in tip submission

I expanded the tipster experience from a single dropdown panel into a series of pages to ensure clarity and avoid overwhelming readers with too many steps at once.

Before

Single tip chat field and tip visibility panel.

After

Multi-page tipster flow shows one task at a time.

Feed Filtering

Giving readers more control over their feed

Accessible filters let readers shape their feed, ensuring relevance without overwhelming choice.

Dropping the regional filter map for simplicity

Despite an early interest in a map that could visualize selected regions, the feature was dropped to avoid unnecessary information and consolidate space on the filters page.

Before

Map visualizes regional filter choices.

After

Map removed to streamline filters page.

Reducing emphasis on filter tags in feed

Filter tags highlight personalization in the feed, but we kept them simple to avoid clutter and to ensure they don’t feel like clickable controls.

Before

Overly prominent filter tags imply interactivity.

After

Subtle filter tags let post content shine.

Results

Outcome

A new product finding it's footing

Through my prototypes, discussions, and presentations, the Chronicle largely validated the need for a robust Openings & Closings vertical. Newsroom leaders are now building on my work to move it toward launch.

Takeaways

Taking a chance and the lead

I touched every aspect of this product precisely because it was an ambiguous space, that seemed interesting but far off to the newsroom. By prototyping I resolved some of that ambiguity, leading to more constructive conversations and clearer goals.

Prototype at a tangible fidelity

I skipped early wireframes to quickly experiment with Chronicle UI patterns and create prototypes stakeholders could engage with meaningfully, without getting bogged down in rough visuals.

Leaning on subject matter experts

I drew on colleagues’ and mentors’ journalism expertise, learning from their first-hand experience in reporting, regional strategy, audience, and monetization, to inform my design decisions.
